My Kathak Journey

They say dance speaks where words fail — and that’s exactly how I feel about Kathak. What started as a rhythmic curiosity two and a half years ago has grown into something far deeper — a silent language, a sacred ritual, a powerful storytelling form that flows through my breath and bones.

I’m currently under the soulful guidance of Guru Sharath R. Prabhath, whose clarity and grace continue to mold me. It’s a blessing to be part of a legacy shaped by Paramaguru Rajendra Gangani and Nirupama Rajendra — visionaries who brought fire, form, and finesse to the stage.

I find beauty in the dialogue between the Lucknow and Jaipur gharanas — softness meets structure, abhinaya meets energy. It’s in this duality that my own style emerges. Whether I’m exploring rhythm or rasa, the form feels infinite.

I recently earned a distinction in ABGMV (Akhil Bharatiya Gandharva Mahavidyalaya) — a small milestone in this long, lifelong journey. Kathak has taught me discipline, humility, joy, and most of all, silence between movement.

Kathak is not just performance. It's prayer. A rebellion. A memory. A mirror.
It's how I speak when words are not enough.
