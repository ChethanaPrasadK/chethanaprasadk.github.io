Hi, I'm Chethana.

I'm a pre-doctoral researcher trying to get better at building machine learning systems that hold up to scrutiny — not just accurate, but private and auditable too. My work sits mostly at the intersection of federated learning, differential privacy, and data governance, with a smaller side project in quantum-resilient aggregation that I'm still early into.

I'm currently at the WSL Lab, IIIT Bangalore, working on Anumati — a consent-management prototype for public data infrastructure, built with an eye on DPDP and GDPR requirements. It's a collaborative effort with IISc-CDPG, CDAC, and IUDX under MoHUA and MeitY, and I'm one of several people contributing to it, not running it solo.

Before this, I spent about eight months at IISc's Centre for Data Science as a Research Associate, where I worked on federated ML algorithms and privacy-preserving aggregation — mainly looking at client heterogeneity and the trade-offs between clustering strategies and privacy guarantees. That work became the basis for my M.Tech thesis on federated GNN aggregation.

I'm also partway through an M.S. in Computer Science at Georgia Tech (the OMSCS program, done remotely), focused on AI with a minor in HCI. It's coursework-heavy rather than research-heavy, but a few of the course projects turned into small publications or working prototypes — I've noted which ones on the Research and Projects pages.

Before moving into research, I worked as a Systems Engineer at Infosys for about two years, mostly on testing and validation for Finacle core banking systems. It wasn't glamorous work, but it taught me a lot about what "production-grade" actually means, which I still think about when I'm tempted to cut corners in research code.

Outside of research, I train in Kathak and passed my ABGMV examination with distinction in 2025. I mention it here because the discipline of practicing something slowly and repeatedly until it's right is the same muscle I use in research — not because I think the two are the same thing.
