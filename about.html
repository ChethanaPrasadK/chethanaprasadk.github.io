Hi, I'm Chethana.

I build machine learning systems that are private, trustworthy, and deployable — not just theoretically elegant. My core research lives at the intersection of federated learning, differential privacy, and data governance, with a growing thread into quantum-resilient aggregation protocols.

Currently, I'm a Pre-Doctoral Researcher at the WSL Lab, IIIT Bangalore, where I'm developing Anumati — a large-scale consent management system for India's national digital data infrastructure under DPDP and GDPR regulatory frameworks. I collaborate with IISc-CDPG, CDAC, and IUDX on initiatives under MoHUA and MeitY.

Before IIIT-B, I spent eight months at IISc Centre for Data Science as a Research Associate, studying federated ML algorithms and privacy-preserving aggregation frameworks — particularly around client heterogeneity, clustering strategies, and performance-privacy trade-offs. That work fed directly into my M.Tech thesis on geometric coherence in heterogeneous federated GNNs.

I'm also completing an M.S. in Computer Science at Georgia Tech (online), specializing in AI with a minor in Human-Computer Interaction. The OMSCS program has pushed me into territory I love: knowledge-based AI, cognitive architectures, AI ethics, and digital health equity. Several of those courses resulted in publications and deployed prototypes.

Before full-time research, I was a Systems Engineer at Infosys, working on testing and validation for Finacle core banking systems. That grounding in enterprise software — where reliability isn't optional — shapes how I think about deploying ML in the real world.

Away from research, I'm a trained Kathak dancer with a Distinction from ABGMV. I believe the discipline of classical dance — rhythm, precision, expression — has more in common with research than people think.
