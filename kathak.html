Beyond Research

The discipline of
Kathak dance.

Rhythm, precision, and expression — the same qualities that define good research also define classical Indian dance.

A practice, not a hobby.

I've been training in Kathak for many years — it is one of the eight classical dance forms of India, originating in the temples and courts of North India and characterized by intricate footwork, expressive gestures (mudras), and rapid spins (chakkar).

Kathak demands the same qualities I value in research: sustained attention, iterative refinement, the ability to communicate complex ideas through precise movements, and the patience to do something wrong many times before you do it right. A tukra practiced 500 times is not unlike a model trained through hundreds of iterations.

In 2025, I earned a Distinction in my Kathak Pariksha from ABGMV (Akhil Bharatiya Gandharva Mahavidyalaya Mandal) — one of India's foremost classical music and dance examination bodies. This is a formal credential that reflects years of structured, graded training and performance evaluation.

I mention Kathak on my academic portfolio not as a curiosity, but because I believe the people who bring the most to research are often those who have deeply pursued something outside of it.
